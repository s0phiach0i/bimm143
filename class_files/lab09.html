Q9. Is the distribution of winpercent values symmetrical? Answer: No, the distribution of winpercent values is not symmetrical. It appears to be slightly right-skewed, meaning there are more candies with lower win percentages than higher ones.

Q10. Is the center of the distribution above or below 50%? Answer: The center of the distribution is below 50%, as the majority of the data tends to cluster in the 40–50% range, pulling the median below the 50% mark.

Q25. Based on your exploratory analysis, correlation findings, and PCA results, what combination of characteristics appears to make a “winning” candy? How do these different analyses (visualization, correlation, PCA) support or complement each other in reaching this conclusion? Answer: Across all three analyses, winning candies share a common profile: they are chocolate-based, bar-shaped, and contain peanuts, almonds, or caramel. The visualization identified top ranked candies like Reese’s Peanut Butter Cup, Twix, Kit Kat, and Snickers as sharing these traits. The correlation matrix showed that chocolate and bar format have strong positive correlations with winpercent, whereas fruity candies consistently scored lower. PCA further supported this, with PC1 clearly dividing chocolate (negative side) and fruity (positive side) candies into opposite ends, with chocolate, bar, and winpercent loading together in the same negative direction. Together, these three methods all point to chocolate as the defining factor of a popular candy.
